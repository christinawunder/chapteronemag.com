Monica, 28, from Bogota

Team of 5, Crisp is the boss

1)What did you study?

Art and design (handcraft and graphic design) to solve human necessity. If you need to sit, you make a chair. If you want to communicate, you make campaigns.

I focuses on sustainable design.

NGO founded with Likmi "projecto Dominga"

Want to help people do art and craft without being exploited. Female collective try to connect female communities also in rural areas. Help them to share knowledge and understand that they are similar.

Lived in the Amazon to write thesis. There Likmi was teaching art, and met Dominga. She wanted to see the sea.takin kids from different communities to meet other kids from different territorities to use art as a tool to connect.

In my studies there areTwo ways: wealthy university, so go abroad and do master, connect with art scene (create your own company) or: not so wealthy, you work freelance for bigger companies. Not well paid.

As young people we always want to go away, but we never focus on our own territories.

We want to help them have a different perspective.

Made a trip with kids from Amazon to Pacific coast on Whale season. Give them experiences thy can change their lives.

Crowdfunding. Artists donated art pieces that were sold, fundraising events

Of course we struggle with the money.

2)What is your job now? / how do you make a living?

Try to develop my art. Guiding, manage the tours. My real job is to manage the tour. So if I do what I wanted. Social projects, designing things, projects. But it is not the creative position I was hoping for. I get by, but I'm not where I wanted to be yet. But the dream job when I was studying changed. It built the path to where I am now.

Contemporary society doesn't really define these jobs.

I like Bogota feel whole country

70% of population came here from rural areas for a better life

100 years 275.000 people living here, now almost 10 mio. So all are second generation immigrants

3) what was the most helpful thing that university thought you which is useful for you in life now?

Think, use knowledge. Critical thinking. I am lucky, I went to good university

Public school and private are very different here. National university is great, but they teach to have employees.

Wealthy class is being taught to develop and improve things. But I struggled with the fact that people were only thinking about money, themselves etc. But not about their community's other people and poor people.

4) what is the coolest part of your job?

Creating projects and seeing them come true

5) what motivates you to keep going when things get more difficult?

I am stubborn, so I don't give up

6) if you can pick a dream: where would you like to see yourself in 5 years? / Bogota graffiti scene in 5 years?

Would love to be selling my designs and have exhibitions, but keep working on social projects, cultural management, so giving opportunities to children and community

7) tips recommendation to your younger self?

Keep focussed. I am not constant. I have skills that I don't exploit to the max because of lack of discipline. Being an artist requires discipline. Not having a formal job has advantages and disadvantages. You can waste your time if you don't have fixed hours.
